Privacy

InfoSphere Privacy Summary

InfoSphere is designed as a privacy-forward prototype for understanding information sources, not for reading private content.

Data Used

InfoSphere uses Android Usage Access only after the user grants permission. This provides app package names, app names, foreground-use windows, and durations.

Optional Connected Data

Users may optionally connect platform accounts. Connected data is used to add aggregate context, such as public channel taxonomy, public followed-account metadata summaries, reviewed ownership-family hints, or public community context.

Data Not Used

InfoSphere does not read private messages, scrape feeds, inspect individual posts or videos, collect comments, collect saved items, collect DMs, collect social passwords, or capture VPN/network traffic.

Storage Boundary

The prototype stores local app usage summaries and approved aggregate account-context fields needed to show the dashboard. Access tokens, raw followed lists, subscription lists, posts, feeds, comments, DMs, and raw platform item lists are not stored as dashboard data.

User Control

Users can disconnect account context, reset local source mappings, clear app data, or uninstall the app to remove locally stored prototype data.
